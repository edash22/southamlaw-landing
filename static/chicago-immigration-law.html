Frequently Asked Questions

How do I immigrate to the US?

The immigration process will look different in each case. You must first have a “connection” to the US. That could be sponsorship from a family member, sponsorship from a company, your own company, approved asylum, or winning the green card lottery. Then you must review your background to see if your history might cause a concern: crimes, entering the US without a visa or staying too long on a visa, lying to government officials, etc.

Can I change to student status?

If you are in the US and in valid status, you can probably change to F-1 student status. Southam Law regularly works with individuals in J-1, B-2, and many other statuses to change to F-1 student status. The key requirements include admission to a school (to get an I-20 form), showing you have finances to pay the school, demonstrating that you have a home outside the US, and showing that you stayed in good status before applying.

Can I get a Green Card through marriage and how long does it take?

If you are married to a US citizen or permanent resident, you can apply for your own green card. The time depends on a number of things like where you live, the current procedures of immigration officials, and luck. The fastest cases take at least six months, most cases take around a year, the longer cases take several years.

Can a Green Card application be denied?

Any application to immigration officials can be denied. Denials are most common if the person applying has a history of falling out of immigration status, criminal history, or some other serious issue. Denials also happen if immigration doesn’t believe your marriage is truthful or if your sponsor doesn’t have enough income to sponsor an immigrant. Consulting with an immigration attorney can help you avoid a denial. And even if your application is denied, you can appeal or file a new application.

Can I apply for asylum?

Asylum is a complex and serious application. If you fear returning to your home country for a good reason, asylum might be right for you. You must show that you are at risk of serious injury or imprisonment. You must also show that the reason someone wants to harm you is due to your politics, religion, skin color, place of birth, language, sexual orientation, or some other fundamental part of your personality that you cannot change. If your asylum is approved, you can remain in the U.S. permanently. If it’s not approved, you risk possible deportation proceedings.

At what point can I get a work permit?

Work permits are available for a variety of reasons under several programs. You must qualify under a specific program. Depending on the program, you may be able to get a work permit in only a few months or it may take many months. If your work permit is approved, you can also get a social security number (SSN).

Do I need a lawyer for my immigration process?

You are not required to have a lawyer. But it’s a good idea. They say there are two areas of law you should always have an expert: taxes and immigration. The reason is that immigration (like taxes) is filling with special programs with special rules and lots of exceptions. At a minimum, it’s a good idea to consult with an immigration attorney before filing anything with government officials.

What is USCIS?

USCIS (United States Citizenship and Immigration Services) is a federal agency in the Department of Homeland Security. They handle most of the benefits of the immigration system (work visas, work permits, etc.).
